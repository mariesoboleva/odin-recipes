Fettuccine Alfredo

Best Chicken Fettuccine Alfredo

This is a classic Italian-American dish that needs to be in every single kitchen. My Fettuccine Alfredo Recipe and Skinny Fettuccine Alfredo Recipe (with 75% less fat) are some of the most popular recipes on my Modern Honey. So I decided to add some protein by adding some sliced chicken breast!

It all starts with a homemade alfredo sauce made with butter, garlic, heavy cream, and parmesan cheese tossed with fettuccine pasta. It is topped with seasoned sauteed chicken breast.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Fettuccine Alfredo (I prefer to use fresh, refrigerated fettuccine pasta)
  • 6 Tablespoons Salted Butter
  • 1 Garlic Clove (minced)
  • 1 ½ cups Heavy Cream
  • 1 ¼ cups Parmesan Cheese (high-quality, grated)
  • ¼ teaspoon Salt
  • ¼ teaspoon Pepper
  • Fresh Parsley (optional)
  • 3 Chicken Breasts
  • 3 Tablespoons Butter
  • ½ teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Parsley
  • ½-1 teaspoon Salt

Steps

  1. While sauce is thickening, heat another skillet over medium-high heat. Add 3 Tablespoons of butter to the pan and let melt. Place the chicken in the pan and sprinkle with garlic powder, parsley, and salt. Cook for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the chicken breast. Cook until no longer pink. Let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
  2. While sauce is thickening, heat another skillet over medium-high heat. Add 3 Tablespoons of butter to the pan and let melt. Place the chicken in the pan and sprinkle with garlic powder, parsley, and salt. Cook for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the chicken breast. Cook until no longer pink. Let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
  3. Let heavy cream reduce and cook for 5 to 8 minutes. Add half of the parmesan cheese to the mixture and whisk well until smooth. Keep over heat and whisk well until cheese is melted.
  4. While sauce is thickening, heat another skillet over medium-high heat. Add 3 Tablespoons of butter to the pan and let melt. Place the chicken in the pan and sprinkle with garlic powder, parsley, and salt. Cook for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the chicken breast. Cook until no longer pink. Let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
  5. Toss alfredo sauce with fettuccine pasta and add half of the parmesan cheese. Once it is tossed, garnish with the remaining parmesan cheese. Add a little pasta water if it needs to be thinned out.
  6. Top with sliced chicken breast and garnish with fresh parsley, if so desired.
